
AGENTIC TRADING NETWORK
Agent Architecture & Workflow Specification
Version 1.0  |  March 2026

Four-Agent Autonomous Trading System
Strategy Builder  .  Backtester  .  Trader  .  Trading Coach


System Overview
This document defines the architecture, responsibilities, decision logic, inputs, outputs, and inter-agent communication protocols for the Agentic Trading Network. The system comprises four specialized agents operating in a continuous feedback loop, designed to autonomously generate, test, execute, and refine trading strategies.

Core Design Philosophy
Separation of concerns -- each agent owns one domain and does it exceptionally well
Risk-first thinking -- every agent considers downside before upside
Continuous learning -- the Trading Coach feeds insights back to the Strategy Builder, creating a self-improving loop
Human oversight -- the system is designed to surface decisions for human review at key checkpoints
Capital preservation -- no single trade should risk the entire account; steady compounding beats aggressive speculation

The Workflow at a Glance
Step 1
Strategy Builder generates 100 candidate strategies per cycle across diverse indicator combinations, timeframes, and risk profiles
Step 2
Backtester evaluates all 100 against historical data using EV scoring, Monte Carlo drawdown matrices, and prop firm safety checks -- selects the top 3 survivors
Step 3
Trader runs all 3 approved strategies concurrently in paper or live markets, each with its own risk allocation
Step 4
Trading Coach monitors performance weekly & monthly, adjusts risk exposure
Step 5
Coach findings feed back to Strategy Builder for next generation of strategies


AGENT 1: STRATEGY BUILDER
Generates candidate trading strategies for backtesting evaluation

Role & Purpose
The Strategy Builder is the creative engine of the network. Its sole purpose is generating well-structured, testable trading strategies by combining technical indicators, risk/reward parameters, entry/exit rules, and market context filters. It does not evaluate strategies -- that is the Backtester's job. It produces, diversifies, and innovates.

Core Responsibilities
Generate a batch of 100 candidate strategies per cycle using maximally varied approaches -- different indicator combinations, timeframes, entry logic, and risk profiles
Ensure no two strategies in a batch are too correlated -- diversification is mandatory
Incorporate feedback from the Trading Coach to refine future strategy generation
Tag each strategy with metadata: market type, timeframe, indicator set, and risk profile
Maintain a strategy memory log to avoid regenerating underperforming approaches

Strategy Components
Technical Indicators
Each strategy must define a primary signal indicator and at least one confirmation indicator. The builder selects from the following categories:
Trend indicators: Moving Averages (SMA, EMA, WMA), MACD, ADX, Supertrend
Momentum indicators: RSI, Stochastic Oscillator, CCI, Williams %R, ROC
Volatility indicators: Bollinger Bands, ATR, Keltner Channels, Standard Deviation
Volume indicators: OBV, VWAP, Volume Profile, Chaikin Money Flow
Support/Resistance: Pivot Points, Fibonacci Retracements, Donchian Channels

Entry & Exit Rules
Every strategy must define precise, unambiguous entry and exit conditions:
Entry Trigger
The exact condition that initiates a trade (e.g., RSI crosses above 30 + price above 50 EMA)
Entry Filter
Secondary confirmation required before entry (e.g., volume above 20-day average)
Stop Loss
Maximum acceptable loss per trade, defined as ATR multiple or fixed percentage
Take Profit
Target exit level, defined as R-multiple (e.g., 2R, 3R) or indicator-based
Trailing Stop
Optional dynamic stop that locks in profits as price moves favorably
Time Exit
Maximum hold duration -- forces exit if neither TP nor SL is reached

Risk & Position Sizing
Risk Per Trade
Chosen by the Strategy Builder per strategy -- tested at 0.5%, 1%, 1.5%, and 2% during backtesting. The Backtester selects whichever level produced the best risk-adjusted return. The 2% cap is absolute and cannot be exceeded under any circumstance.
Position Size
Calculated from the strategy's chosen risk amount and the distance to the stop loss -- never manually overridden
Max Open Positions
Maximum concurrent trades -- default 3, never exceeds 5
Correlation Limit
No two open positions in highly correlated assets simultaneously
Daily Loss Limit
If daily drawdown exceeds 3%, no new trades opened that day

Strategy Output Format
Each strategy is output as a structured JSON object containing all parameters required for backtesting. This ensures the Backtester can run it without ambiguity or interpretation.

Inputs Received
Trading Coach feedback report (weekly/monthly performance analysis)
Historical performance log of previously generated strategies
Current market regime classification (trending, ranging, volatile)
Asset universe and timeframes to focus on for this cycle

Outputs Produced
Batch of 10 fully parameterized strategy JSON objects
Strategy metadata tags for each candidate
Diversity score confirming low correlation between batch strategies


AGENT 2: STRATEGY BACKTESTER
Evaluates 100 candidate strategies and selects the top 3 performers using Monte Carlo drawdown matrices

Role & Purpose
The Strategy Backtester is the quality gate of the network. It receives 100 candidate strategies from the Builder, runs each against historical market data, applies hard elimination filters, and then ranks survivors using the Monte Carlo drawdown matrices and EV scoring framework. The top 3 strategies -- those with the strongest risk-adjusted returns, lowest 95th percentile drawdown, and best prop firm safety classification -- are selected and passed to the Trader for concurrent deployment. A 3-in-100 selection rate ensures only genuinely exceptional strategies reach live markets.

Core Responsibilities
Run all 100 candidate strategies against historical price data (minimum 2 years) in parallel batches
Apply hard elimination filters -- any strategy failing any hard limit is immediately disqualified
Score all surviving strategies using the Monte Carlo drawdown matrix classifications and EV framework
Rank survivors and select the top 3 -- ensuring the 3 selected strategies are not highly correlated with each other
Produce a detailed evaluation report explaining the top 3 selections and key eliminations
Pass all 3 winning strategies and their full scorecards to the Trader for concurrent deployment

Backtesting Methodology
Data Requirements
Lookback Period
Minimum 2 years of historical OHLCV data
Data Source
Yahoo Finance (free) or Alpha Vantage -- daily and intraday bars
Market Conditions
Test must include at least one bull, one bear, and one sideways period
Out-of-Sample Test
Final 20% of data reserved as unseen test set -- no optimization allowed on it
Slippage Model
0.1% slippage applied per trade to simulate real execution
Commission Model
Fixed $1 per trade or 0.05% -- whichever is higher

Hard Elimination Filters
Any strategy failing ANY of these criteria is immediately disqualified, regardless of other metrics:
Maximum drawdown exceeds 20% of starting capital
Win rate below 35% (statistically insufficient edge)
Fewer than 30 trades in backtest period (insufficient sample size)
Any single trade loss exceeds 5% of account equity -- this applies regardless of what risk per trade level was selected
Consecutive losing streak exceeds 8 trades
Sharpe Ratio below 0.5
Strategy only performs well at the 2% risk level -- if it requires maximum risk to look profitable, it is disqualified

Expected Value Calculation
Before a strategy can be scored, it must demonstrate a positive Expected Value (EV). EV is the mathematical edge of a strategy -- the average outcome per trade expressed as a percentage of the amount risked. A strategy with negative EV is a losing strategy regardless of how it looks on any other metric.
EV Formula
EV = (Win Rate ? Reward) - (Loss Rate ? Risk) -- expressed as % of risk per trade
Example: 45% WR at 2R
EV = (0.45 ? 2) - (0.55 ? 1) = +0.35R per trade, or +35% of risk
Break-even Win Rate
The minimum win rate needed at a given R:R = 1 / (1 + R:R). At 2R this is 33.3%, at 1R this is 50%
Profit Factor
Gross profit divided by gross loss. Must exceed 1.5 to pass. Calculated as: (WR ? R:R) / (1 - WR)
EV Disqualification
Any strategy with EV <= 0 is immediately disqualified -- no further scoring occurs

Win Rate Reality Check
Not all win rates are equal. The Backtester applies a reality check to understand what each win rate actually requires:
30% Win Rate
Requires minimum 2.33R to break even. Viable as a sniper strategy but needs very high R:R. Max drawdown at 2R is typically high.
40% Win Rate
Requires 1.5R minimum. Achievable but demands disciplined R:R management. Preferred floor for this system.
45% Win Rate
Solid edge with 2R+. This is the target baseline for strategies entering scoring.
50% Win Rate
Coin flip baseline -- needs any positive R:R to be profitable. Breaks even at 1:1.
55%+ Win Rate
Above average. Verify sample size is sufficient -- high win rates on small samples are statistically suspect.
60%+ Win Rate
Requires validation -- abnormally high win rates on backtests often indicate curve-fitting to historical data.

Monte Carlo Drawdown Analysis
Rather than relying on a single backtest path, the Backtester runs 800 Monte Carlo simulations for each strategy to understand the full range of possible drawdown outcomes. This reveals the statistical worst case, not just the historical one.
Simulation Count
800 independent simulations per strategy -- sufficient for stable 95th percentile estimates
95th Percentile DD
The drawdown that would be exceeded only 5% of the time -- used as the risk benchmark, not the average
Trade Sample
Simulations run over 100 trades minimum -- matches typical monthly exposure
Risk Levels Tested
Each strategy is simulated at its chosen risk per trade (0.5% to 2%)
Max Consecutive Losses
Estimated as: log((1 - Loss Rate) ? N) / log(1 / Loss Rate) + 2 -- the expected worst losing streak
Recovery Requirement
Calculated as: DD% / (100 - DD%) ? 100 -- how much gain is needed to recover from the drawdown

Drawdown Risk Classification
Each strategy receives a drawdown risk classification based on its 95th percentile Monte Carlo result:
Low Risk (DD < 4%)
Acceptable for all account types. Green light for deployment.
Moderate Risk (4-7%)
Manageable. Passes most prop firm rules. Proceed with monitoring.
High Risk (7-12%)
Approaches prop firm limits. Reduce position sizing or reconsider.
Extreme Risk (DD > 12%)
Disqualified. Recovery requirement becomes unreasonable and violates most prop firm rules.

Prop Firm Safety Check
Many traders operate under prop firm rules with defined maximum drawdown limits. The Backtester automatically checks each strategy against three standard prop firm thresholds:
5% DD Rule
Strictest -- many evaluation accounts use this. Strategy must show 95th pct DD below 5% to pass.
8% DD Rule
Standard prop firm maximum daily/weekly drawdown rule.
10% DD Rule
Most common overall account drawdown limit across prop firms.
Result
Strategies are flagged with which rule they breach first, or marked as passing all three. Only strategies passing the 8% rule or better are eligible for selection.

Scoring Model
Strategies surviving all hard filters and EV checks are scored across six weighted dimensions:
Expected Value Score (25%)
EV per trade as % of risk, weighted by profit factor -- higher is better. Minimum EV of +20% of risk required to score.
Drawdown Profile (25%)
95th percentile Monte Carlo DD score -- lower is better. Weighted by recovery requirement.
Risk-Adjusted Return (20%)
Sharpe Ratio and Sortino Ratio -- rewards return per unit of volatility taken
Consistency (15%)
Monthly return variance and win rate stability across bull, bear, and sideways periods
Profit Factor (10%)
Gross profit divided by gross loss -- minimum 1.5 required, higher scores better
Statistical Confidence (5%)
Trade count relative to minimum sample size. More trades = more reliable edge estimate.

Return Projection
Each of the top 3 strategies receives a projected monthly return across 3, 6, 12, and 24 month horizons under three compounding scenarios -- no compounding, monthly rebasing, and per-trade compounding. These projections are passed to the Trading Coach as individual performance benchmarks. If any strategy's live performance falls below 50% of its projected return over any monthly period, the Coach flags it for review and potential replacement.

Matrix-Based Selection Logic
After scoring, the top 3 strategies are selected using a two-stage process. First, the highest-scoring strategy is chosen. Second, the next highest-scoring strategy that is NOT highly correlated with the first is selected. Third, the next highest-scoring strategy that is NOT highly correlated with either of the first two is selected. This ensures the 3 deployed strategies complement each other rather than amplifying the same risk. If a top-ranked strategy is materially similar to one already deployed and underperforming, it is skipped and the next ranked strategy is considered. The prop firm safety matrix classification from the drawdown matrices is the final arbiter -- all 3 selected strategies must sit in the green or yellow zone at their chosen risk level.

Inputs Received
100 candidate strategy JSON objects from the Strategy Builder
Historical OHLCV market data for the relevant assets and timeframes
Historical performance log of previously deployed strategies

Outputs Produced
Full scorecard for all 100 strategies with elimination reasons (passed to Trading Coach for reference)
Top 3 winning strategy JSONs with complete performance metrics and matrix classifications
Elimination report summarising the most common failure reasons across the 100 candidates
Confidence rating for each of the 3 selected strategies (High / Medium / Low)
Drawdown matrix classification for each selected strategy at its chosen risk level


AGENT 3: TRADER
Executes approved strategies in live or paper markets with precision

Role & Purpose
The Trader is the execution arm of the network. It receives the Backtester's top 3 selected strategies and runs all three concurrently in real or simulated markets. Each strategy operates independently with its own risk allocation, position tracking, and performance log. The Trader does not deviate from any strategy, does not make discretionary decisions, and does not override risk parameters. Its job is flawless parallel execution, complete per-strategy trade logging, and surfacing real-world performance data for the Trading Coach.

Core Responsibilities
Load all 3 approved strategies and their parameters before market open each day
Run all 3 strategies concurrently -- each monitored independently for entry triggers
Manage open positions for each strategy according to its own stop loss, take profit, and trailing stop rules
Track risk budget separately per strategy -- each strategy has its own daily loss limit
Enforce total account risk limit across all 3 strategies combined -- no single day can lose more than 5% across all active strategies
Log every trade in full detail per strategy: entry time, exit time, price, size, P&L, reason for exit, which strategy triggered it
Report daily and weekly performance summaries per strategy to the Trading Coach
Flag anomalies -- if market conditions change materially for any strategy, escalate to human review

Risk Management Philosophy
The Trader follows the risk management settings defined in each individual strategy -- not a generic set of defaults. This is critical: the Backtester already optimized and validated those specific risk parameters. If the Trader overrides them, it is no longer executing the tested strategy. That would invalidate the backtest entirely.
With 3 strategies running concurrently, total account exposure must be managed carefully. Each strategy's risk per trade is capped at its backtested level, and the combined daily loss limit across all 3 strategies is 5% -- not 5% per strategy. Hard safety overrides are non-negotiable regardless of what any strategy instructs:
Per-Strategy Risk Settings
The Trader reads and follows each strategy's individual: risk per trade %, stop loss, take profit, trailing stop, max concurrent positions, and entry/exit timing
Combined Exposure Limit
Total open risk across all 3 strategies cannot exceed 6% of account equity at any time -- prevents over-exposure when all 3 strategies enter simultaneously
Hard Override: Daily Halt
If total account equity drops 5% across all strategies in a single day, all trading halts -- no exceptions
Hard Override: Weekly Reduction
If combined weekly drawdown reaches 8%, position sizes are cut by 50% across all strategies for the remainder of the week
Hard Override: Single Trade Cap
No single trade from any strategy may risk more than 2% of account equity -- network-level rule, cannot be overridden
Hard Override: Strategy Isolation
If one strategy triggers a daily halt individually (3% single-strategy daily loss), that strategy pauses -- the other two continue unaffected
Hard Override: Regime Change
If the Trading Coach flags a major market regime shift, the Trader pauses new entries across all strategies until cleared
Hard Override: API Failure
If broker connection is lost, all open positions across all strategies are closed if possible and all activity halts

Pre-Trade Checklist
Before executing any trade from any strategy, the Trader verifies all of the following:
Strategy Loaded
All parameters confirmed and loaded from Backtester output for this specific strategy
Per-Strategy Risk Budget
Confirm this strategy's individual daily loss limit has not been reached
Combined Risk Budget
Confirm total account exposure from all 3 strategies combined is within the 6% limit
Position Limit Check
Confirm open position count for this strategy is below its maximum
Correlation Check
Confirm new trade is not highly correlated with any existing open position across all 3 strategies
Market Hours
Confirm market is open and liquidity is sufficient
News Filter
Avoid entry within 30 minutes of major economic announcements

Trade Execution Rules
Entry orders are limit orders by default -- market orders only if price moves away within 5 seconds
Stop loss is placed immediately upon entry -- never a mental stop
Take profit target is set immediately upon entry
Position size is calculated precisely from each strategy's risk per trade parameter, never rounded up
Each strategy's trades are tagged with a strategy ID in the log for clean per-strategy performance attribution
Partial exits are permitted if a strategy defines scaling out rules

Risk Management During Execution
Combined Daily Hard Stop
5% total account drop across all strategies in one day -- halts all three immediately
Per-Strategy Daily Pause
3% single-strategy daily loss -- pauses that strategy only, others continue
Weekly Combined Limit
8% combined weekly drawdown -- reduces position sizes by 50% across all strategies for the week
Strategy Invalidation
If market regime shifts dramatically for one strategy's asset class, pause that strategy and flag for Coach review
Broker API Failure
Connection lost -- close all positions across all strategies if possible, then halt

Supported Broker Integration
Alpaca Markets -- paper and live trading via REST API (recommended for development)
Interactive Brokers -- via IBKR API for professional deployments
Extensible to any broker with a REST or WebSocket API

Inputs Received
Top 3 strategy JSONs from the Backtester with full performance metrics and matrix classifications
Real-time market data feed (price, volume, indicators) for all active strategy assets
Per-strategy risk adjustment instructions from the Trading Coach

Outputs Produced
Complete per-strategy trade log with full entry/exit details for every trade
Daily P&L summary broken down by strategy
Weekly performance report per strategy delivered to the Trading Coach
Combined account exposure report showing total risk across all 3 strategies
Anomaly alerts per strategy for human review when conditions warrant


AGENT 4: TRADING COACH
Monitors performance, manages risk exposure, and drives continuous improvement

Role & Purpose
The Trading Coach is the strategic intelligence layer of the network. While the other agents execute a defined process, the Coach steps back and asks: is this working? It reviews performance across all active strategies on a weekly and monthly basis, identifies what is working and what is not, adjusts risk exposure dynamically, and feeds actionable insights back to the Strategy Builder to improve the next generation of strategies. It is the closest thing to a human fund manager in the system.

Core Responsibilities
Review weekly and monthly performance across all active trading strategies
Identify the lowest-performing strategies and implement risk reduction measures
Identify highest-performing strategies and recommend increased exposure where appropriate
Produce a structured feedback report for the Strategy Builder each cycle
Monitor for strategy decay -- a previously good strategy losing its edge over time
Maintain the performance history log used by all other agents
Flag strategies for retirement when they no longer meet performance thresholds

Performance Review Framework
Weekly Review
Every week the Trading Coach evaluates each active strategy across the following metrics:
Weekly P&L
Absolute and percentage return for the week
Live Win Rate vs Expected
Actual win rate this week vs the backtested win rate. Divergence of more than 10 percentage points triggers a review.
Live EV vs Expected
Actual EV per trade this week vs the projected EV from the Backtester. If live EV turns negative for the week, flag immediately.
Average R-Multiple
Average reward relative to risk per trade this week -- is the strategy achieving its targeted R:R?
Max Drawdown (Weekly)
Largest peak-to-trough decline this week vs the 95th percentile Monte Carlo estimate
Trade Count
Actual trade frequency vs expected -- divergence may signal market regime change
Execution Quality
Slippage analysis -- are live results matching backtest expectations?

Monthly Review
Monthly reviews go deeper and drive the major strategic decisions:
Monthly P&L vs Projection
Total return for the month vs the Backtester's projected return benchmark. If below 50% of projection for 2 consecutive months, flag for retirement review.
Live EV vs Backtested EV
Compare actual monthly EV per trade against the backtested EV. A sustained gap signals edge decay or changed market conditions.
Sharpe Ratio (Live)
Compare live Sharpe to backtest Sharpe -- significant gap signals strategy decay
Monte Carlo Comparison
Run updated Monte Carlo on live trade data. If 95th pct drawdown has worsened materially vs backtest estimate, reduce exposure.
Drawdown Analysis
Month's maximum drawdown vs 95th percentile Monte Carlo estimate and prop firm rule thresholds
Strategy Ranking
Rank all active strategies by risk-adjusted monthly EV -- drives exposure adjustment decisions
Win Rate Drift
Is the win rate trending down over time? Consistent decline signals the strategy's edge is eroding.
Regime Analysis
Has market regime changed? Are strategies still appropriate for current conditions?

Risk Exposure Adjustment Rules
The Trading Coach applies the following rules to adjust exposure after each monthly review:
Bottom 25% of strategies by monthly performance: reduce position sizing by 25%
Any strategy with monthly drawdown exceeding 15%: reduce position sizing by 50%
Any strategy with 3 consecutive losing weeks: flag for retirement review
Top 25% of strategies by monthly performance: increase position sizing by up to 25% (never exceeding max position size limits)
Exposure increases are gradual -- never more than 25% increase per review cycle
Retired strategies are logged with full post-mortem analysis for Strategy Builder reference

Feedback Report to Strategy Builder
Each cycle the Trading Coach produces a structured feedback report containing:
What indicator combinations have performed best in current market conditions
What risk/reward ratios have produced the best actual outcomes
What market regimes current strategies are failing in -- focus area for new strategies
Specific hypothesis for the next batch: e.g., 'test mean-reversion in low-volatility conditions'
List of retired strategy patterns to avoid regenerating

Inputs Received
Weekly and daily trade logs from the Trader
Full scorecard history from the Backtester for all previously evaluated strategies
Current market regime data

Outputs Produced
Weekly performance dashboard (summary for human review)
Monthly strategic review report with risk adjustment recommendations
Structured feedback report delivered to Strategy Builder each cycle
Retirement notices for underperforming strategies
Risk adjustment instructions delivered to the Trader


Strategy Health Scoring Framework
This section defines the complete framework for classifying whether a trading strategy is good, marginal, or poor. It is used by both the Backtester (to select winning strategies) and the Trading Coach (to monitor live strategies and decide when to retire them). All metrics derive from the Expected Value, Monte Carlo Drawdown, and Return Projection logic built into the system.

The Three Classification Tiers
Tier
Backtester Criteria
Trading Coach Live Criteria
GOOD
EV > +35% of risk . Profit Factor > 1.8 . 95th pct DD < 7% . Sharpe > 1.0 . Win rate >= 40% . Passes 8% prop rule . Live EV >= 80% of projected
Live EV >= 80% of backtested EV . Win rate within 10 pts of backtest . Monthly return >= 50% of projection . DD within Monte Carlo 95th pct . No consecutive losing weeks > 2
MARGINAL
EV between +15-35% . Profit Factor 1.5-1.8 . 95th pct DD 7-12% . Sharpe 0.5-1.0 . Passes 10% prop rule only . Eligible but not preferred
Live EV 50-80% of backtested EV . Win rate drifted 10-15 pts below backtest . Monthly return 25-50% of projection . Exposure reduced 25% . Coach monitoring intensified
POOR
EV <= 0 or Profit Factor < 1.5 . 95th pct DD > 12% . Sharpe < 0.5 . Win rate < 35% . Fails prop firm rules . Requires 2% risk to look profitable
Live EV negative for the month . Win rate drifted > 15 pts below backtest . Monthly return < 25% of projection . 3+ consecutive losing weeks . Flagged for retirement

Expected Value Deep Dive
EV is the single most important metric for classifying a strategy. A strategy can have a great win rate and still be a losing strategy if the R:R is wrong. Conversely, a strategy with a low win rate can be highly profitable with the right R:R. The table below shows the break-even win rate required at each R:R ratio:
0.5R (1:0.5)
Must win 66.7% of trades to break even. Almost never viable.
1R (1:1)
Must win 50% to break even. Viable but requires above-average win rate.
1.5R (1:1.5)
Must win 40% to break even. Good baseline for trend-following strategies.
2R (1:2)
Must win 33.3% to break even. The preferred minimum R:R for this system.
3R (1:3)
Must win 25% to break even. Excellent R:R -- allows low win rate sniper approaches.
5R (1:5)
Must win 16.7% to break even. High-conviction setups only.
10R (1:10)
Must win 9.1% to break even. Very rare entries, very high reward setups.

Reading the Matrices -- Key Takeaways
These six matrices together tell a clear story about what makes a strategy viable:
At 0.25% risk most strategy combinations are safe -- use this risk level exclusively during prop firm challenge phases and initial live validation
At 0.5% risk you need at minimum 45% win rate and 1:2 R:R to pass the 8% prop firm rule -- this is the recommended risk level for early live deployment
At 1% risk virtually nothing passes prop firm rules -- 1% is a mature live trading risk level only, after a strategy has 3+ months of validated performance
Low R:R strategies (0.5R, 1:1) are dangerous at any risk level unless win rate is extremely high -- the Backtester should rarely select these
High R:R strategies (1:3, 1:5, 1:10) dramatically reduce drawdown even at lower win rates -- the Strategy Builder should bias toward higher R:R setups
The green zone (DD below 5%) is the target for all deployed strategies -- anything yellow or above requires risk reduction before deployment

Consecutive Loss Streak Estimation
One of the most psychologically damaging aspects of any trading strategy is the losing streak. The system estimates the expected maximum consecutive loss streak for each strategy using the formula: log((1 - Loss Rate) x N) / log(1 / Loss Rate) + 2. This is surfaced prominently in the Backtester output and the Trading Coach's weekly report so the Trader can prepare for and withstand streaks without abandoning a strategy prematurely.

Compounding Impact on Strategy Selection
The Backtester projects each strategy's return under three compounding scenarios. These projections inform the Trading Coach's performance benchmarks and the decision of when to increase or decrease exposure to a given strategy:
No Compounding
Risk amount is fixed at starting capital. Predictable, linear growth. Safest approach while validating a strategy in live conditions.
Monthly Compounding
Risk amount rebased to current account balance monthly. Accelerates gains in winning periods but also accelerates drawdowns. Recommended once a strategy has 3+ months of live validation.
Per-Trade Compounding
Risk % applied to balance after every single trade. Maximum growth potential but maximum drawdown amplification. Reserved for strategies with strong live track record only.
Coach Rule
New strategies start on no-compounding. Monthly compounding is unlocked after 3 months of Good-tier performance. Per-trade compounding requires 6 months of Good-tier performance.


Blockchain Data Architecture
Your existing backtester already demonstrates the correct approach to sourcing on-chain data. This section formalises how that data pipeline integrates into the agent network, which chains are supported, and how data quality is managed.

Why On-Chain Data Over Traditional Sources
On-chain subgraph data has several advantages over traditional price feeds like Yahoo Finance or CoinGecko for crypto assets. It is built from every individual swap event recorded on the blockchain, which means it can produce highly accurate VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price) candles rather than simple last-price snapshots. Large high-volume trades dominate the candle price, and thin-liquidity outlier swaps are automatically suppressed because they carry negligible weight. This produces cleaner, more realistic historical data for backtesting.

Fallback: GeckoTerminal OHLCV API
When a subgraph API key is unavailable or a chain has no subgraph, the system falls back to GeckoTerminal's public OHLCV endpoint. This provides pre-aggregated candle data rather than raw swaps, which means VWAP cannot be applied -- thin-liquidity price spikes in the high/low extremes may still appear. GeckoTerminal is limited to approximately 3 months of hourly history, which is insufficient for meaningful multi-year backtesting. Use subgraph data wherever possible.

On the Genesis Block Question
Sourcing data from a coin's genesis block is technically possible and your backtester already supports it via the subgraph approach -- all swaps from the first transaction onward are available. However, using genesis-to-present data for backtesting decisions requires careful judgement:
Early trading periods (typically the first 1-3 months) have near-zero liquidity. Prices can move dramatically on tiny trades, creating ATH readings and Fibonacci levels that are artifacts of illiquidity rather than genuine market discovery.
The trading dynamics of a newly launched token bear little resemblance to how it trades once liquidity matures. Strategies trained on early chaos may perform poorly in normal conditions.
The data quality analyser in your existing backtester already flags this -- it detects candles where high/low ratios exceed 5x, which is the signature of thin-liquidity distortion.
Recommended approach: fetch full history from genesis for completeness and research purposes, but set the backtesting window to begin once minimum liquidity thresholds are met -- for example, once 30-day average daily volume exceeds $50,000 USD.

VWAP Candle Construction
Both the PulseX and Uniswap v3 data paths construct candles using Volume-Weighted Average Price rather than simple last-price. This is the same methodology used in your existing backtester and should be carried forward into the agent network:
Open
First swap's raw price in the time bucket (chronological entry point)
High
Highest raw swap price seen in the bucket -- preserved for sell trigger detection
Low
Lowest raw swap price seen in the bucket -- preserved for buy trigger detection
Close (VWAP)
Sum of (price ? USD volume) divided by total USD volume -- large trades dominate, small outlier swaps are suppressed
Volume
Sum of amountUSD across all swaps in the bucket
Swap Count
Number of individual swaps in the bucket -- low counts flag sparse liquidity periods

Pair Auto-Detection
Rather than requiring a pair address, the system takes a token contract address and automatically finds the best trading pair. The selection logic mirrors your backtester: prefer stablecoin pairs first (most accurate USD pricing), then wrapped native token pairs, then any other pair ranked by volume or TVL. This means the Backtester always prices strategies in USD terms regardless of what the token's primary pair is.

Data Quality Checks
Before any strategy is backtested, the data pipeline runs an automated quality analysis -- the same checks implemented in your existing backtester:
Intra-candle high/low ratio check: flags candles where the high is more than 5x the low, indicating thin-liquidity price spikes
Candle-to-candle VWAP jump check: flags consecutive candles where the close price jumps more than 10x, which can create false ATH readings
Single-swap candle density: if more than 30% of candles contain only one swap, VWAP provides no smoothing -- recommends using a larger time bucket
Any data quality warning is surfaced to the Trading Coach and logged with the backtest results -- strategies run on flagged data are assigned a lower confidence rating
